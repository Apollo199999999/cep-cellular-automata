About

This is a modified version of Conway's Game of Life, with the following rules:

  • Birth: A dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes alive (reproduction).
  • Survival: A live cell with two or three live neighbours stays alive (stasis).
  • Overpopulation: If the cell has 4-8 neighbours, its chance of dying ranges from 20% to 100%.
  • Loneliness: If the cell has 0-1 neighbours, its chance of dying ranges from 0% to 100%.
  • Appearance: If the cell survives either overpopulation or loneliness, the colour of the cell will represent its chance of death previously.

This game also generates music using the dead/alive cells. For every column in the 2D grid, the colour of each cell will be mapped to a note frequency, and all the cells in each column of the 2D grid are then played as one chord. To play the sound, click the corresponding buttons under the "Controls" section.

How to use:

  • Use the "Controls" section to control the game.
  • To make a cell alive, pause the game and click on a dead cell to make it become alive.
  • You can generate music using the dead/alive cells by clicking the corresponding buttons under the "Controls" section.
